A large part of achieving both personal and professional success is learning the skill to resolve conflicts. Conflicts will always occur in life. It could be between you and a coworker, a family member, a friend. It is never fun when you have negative thoughts brewing about someone close to you. However, this wikiHow article will show you how best to resolve any disagreements you may have with someone.

Step 1
Identifying the problem.

  1. 1
    Identify the conflict and its cause. Why are you and your friend disagreeing with each other? Are you stressed out or on edge about something? Think about the circumstances in which you and your friend are in; is there any negative energy surrounding either of you? Identifying the conflict and the cause of it is the first step to making up.
    • Talk to your friend about what you think might be the problem. Communication is very important when it comes to resolving interpersonal issues.

Step 2
Resolving the problem.

  1. 2
    Try talking it out and forgiving each other. Remember, communication is key! Try understanding each otherâ€™s points of view and come to a mutual understanding of the situation. Make an effort to listen to what the other person is saying. Thinking from the other person's perspective as well will also shed light on explaining why they acted a certain way.
    • Instead of thinking of it as 'you VS them', think of it as 'us VS the problem'! Adopting this mindset will help you think of the situation in terms of the problem, rather than the other person.
    • It is okay if it takes time to forgive! Forgiveness does not come easily, and if your 'friend' has done something bad, you do not owe them forgiveness.
